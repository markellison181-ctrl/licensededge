## 🆕 New Licenses Issued This Week

### Ohio — 14 new dispensary licenses
Ohio's Division of Cannabis Control approved 14 new dispensary certificates of operation, bringing the state's total to 144 active retail locations. The state continues its rapid adult-use rollout following the November 2023 ballot initiative. Notable: 5 of the 14 went to social equity applicants.

### Florida — 7 cultivation license amendments
The Florida Office of Medical Marijuana Use (OMMU) approved expanded cultivation capacity for 7 existing MMTCs (Medical Marijuana Treatment Centers). No new operator licenses were issued — Florida's vertically integrated, limited-license model remains among the most restrictive in the country. Total active MMTCs: 23.

### New York — 3 CAURD dispensary openings
Three new Conditional Adult-Use Retail Dispensary (CAURD) locations opened in the NYC metro area (Brooklyn, Queens, Bronx), bringing the total licensed dispensaries in New York to 118. The state's illicit market continues to dwarf legal sales — estimated 3:1 ratio.

### Missouri — 8 micro-licenses approved
Missouri's Division of Cannabis Regulation approved 8 new microbusiness licenses (cultivation + manufacturing), part of the state's social equity program under Amendment 3. These smaller-scale licenses allow up to 250 flowering plants and are capped at $50K in annual licensing fees.

### Minnesota — 2 provisional cultivation licenses
Minnesota's Office of Cannabis Management issued its first 2 provisional cultivation licenses under the adult-use program. Commercial sales remain on track for a mid-2026 launch. These cultivators can begin facility build-out but cannot commence growing until final inspection.

## ⚖️ Regulatory Tracker

### Federal
- **DEA Rescheduling:** Still pending. The DEA has not issued a final rule on marijuana rescheduling from Schedule I to Schedule III, despite the August 2024 proposed rule. Industry sources suggest the Trump administration is deprioritizing the rulemaking. **Our read:** Don't hold your breath in 2026.

- **280E Tax Relief:** With rescheduling stalled, cannabis operators remain subject to IRC Section 280E, which prohibits deductions for businesses trafficking in Schedule I/II substances. However, as Cannabis Industry Journal reports, savvy operators are using ESOP structures to achieve company-level tax exemption when 100% ESOP-owned — an emerging workaround worth watching.

- **SAFER Banking Act:** Dead in the current Congress. No reintroduction expected before 2027 at the earliest. Cannabis companies continue to rely on state-chartered banks, credit unions, and fintech workarounds.

## 🔍 Market Spotlight: Ohio

Ohio is the most important story in cannabis right now. Here's why:

**The numbers:**
- Population: 11.8M (7th largest state)
- Adult-use sales since launch (Aug. 2024): $1.9B+
- Monthly run rate (Jan. 2026): ~$175M
- Active dispensaries: 144 (up from 97 at adult-use launch)
- Active cultivators: 58
- Average dispensary revenue: ~$1.2M/month

**Why Ohio matters for the industry:**

1. **It's the test case for Midwest cannabis.** Ohio is the first large Midwestern state with a voter-approved adult-use program. Its success (or failure) directly influences the political calculus in Pennsylvania, Indiana, and Wisconsin.

2. **The social equity program is actually working.** Ohio allocated 36% of new licenses to social equity applicants — and unlike New York's disastrous rollout, it backed the program with $50M in technical assistance and low-interest loans. Five of this week's 14 new licenses went to social equity operators.

3. **Price compression is happening faster than expected.** Average retail prices dropped 22% YoY (from $42/eighth to $33/eighth) as new cultivation capacity came online. This is squeezing smaller operators but making the legal market competitive with the illicit market — the holy grail for regulators.

4. **Interstate commerce implications.** Ohio's border-state dynamics are fascinating. Michigan (legal) is to the north, Pennsylvania (medical-only, but adult-use bill advancing) to the east, Kentucky (medical launching 2027) to the south, and Indiana (fully illegal) to the west. Ohio dispensaries near the Indiana and Kentucky borders are reporting 30–40% of customers from out of state.

**Investment thesis:** Ohio MSOs with strong dispensary portfolios (Curaleaf, Green Thumb Industries, Verano) are well-positioned, but the real opportunity may be in Ohio-based single-state operators that could get acquired as larger players consolidate.
